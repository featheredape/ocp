Salt Spring Island OCP — External Regulatory Document Reference List
====================================================================
Prepared: February 24, 2026

This list identifies the key external regulatory documents from the Islands
Trust and the Capital Regional District (CRD) that govern or interact with
the Salt Spring Island Official Community Plan (Bylaw No. 434, 2008).

Note: Direct PDF downloads from islandstrust.bc.ca and crd.ca were blocked
by the network proxy during this session. URLs are provided for manual
download. Content was obtained through web search results and verified
against multiple sources.

=====================================
ISLANDS TRUST DOCUMENTS
=====================================

1. ISLANDS TRUST POLICY STATEMENT — Bylaw No. 17
   Status: Current (adopted 1994, consolidated 2003, in force)
   URL: https://islandstrust.bc.ca/document/islands-trust-policy-statement-bylaw-no-17/
   Alt: https://islandstrust.bc.ca/document/islands-trust-policy-statement-consolidated-2023/
   Relevance: CRITICAL — The overarching policy framework. Under s.15 of the
   Islands Trust Act, the OCP must not be "contrary to or at variance with"
   this Statement. Contains directive policies that local trust committees
   "shall" address in OCPs and regulatory bylaws.

2. ISLANDS TRUST POLICY STATEMENT DRAFT — Bylaw No. 183
   Status: Draft (First Reading July 29, 2025; Phase 4 public engagement
           ongoing to Feb 2026)
   URL: https://www.friendsofthegulfislands.ca/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/2024-12-3-TPS-draft.pdf
   Alt: https://sd46.diligent.community/document/797e9b23-58cc-49b1-847f-f97b84923bb0/
   Relevance: HIGH — Proposed replacement for Bylaw 17. Significantly
   strengthens directives on housing, ecosystems, freshwater, reconciliation,
   and climate change. Will require OCP amendments upon adoption.

3. SALT SPRING ISLAND LAND USE BYLAW — Bylaw No. 355
   Status: Current (consolidated Sept 2020, with subsequent amendments)
   URL: https://islandstrust.bc.ca/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/bl-355_lub_2020-09.pdf
   Alt: https://islandstrust.bc.ca/document/salt-spring-island-land-use-bylaw-no-2023/
   Relevance: CRITICAL — The zoning bylaw that implements the OCP.
   Establishes permitted uses, densities, setbacks, and subdivision
   requirements for every parcel on the island. Inconsistencies between
   OCP designations and LUB zones directly affect property owners.

4. ISLANDS TRUST ACT — RSBC 1996, c. 239
   Status: Current provincial legislation
   URL: https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/civix/document/id/complete/statreg/96239_01
   Alt: https://www.canlii.org/en/bc/laws/stat/rsbc-1996-c-239/latest/rsbc-1996-c-239.html
   Relevance: HIGH — Establishes the legal framework: the "object" of the
   Trust (s.3), Policy Statement requirements (s.15), and the authority and
   obligations of local trust committees.

=====================================
CAPITAL REGIONAL DISTRICT DOCUMENTS
=====================================

5. CRD BUILDING REGULATION BYLAW — Bylaw No. 3741
   Status: Current (consolidated Feb 2023)
   URL: https://www.crd.ca/media/file/crdbylaw3741consolidated8feb2023
   Relevance: MODERATE — Regulates building permits, inspections, and
   construction standards in the Salt Spring Island Electoral Area.
   Interacts with OCP energy efficiency objectives (A.6) and building
   design guidelines (E.1).

6. CRD ELECTORAL AREAS WATER CONSERVATION BYLAW — Bylaw No. 4492
   Status: Current (adopted 2023, amended 2025; effective May 1–Sept 30)
   URL: https://www.crd.ca/media/file/bylawno4492consolidatedapril2025
   Relevance: HIGH — Regulates water use in CRD water systems on Salt Spring
   (Highland-Fernwood, Fulford, Cedars of Tuam). Establishes 4-stage
   restriction system. Interacts directly with OCP water policies (C.3)
   and density provisions linked to water supply.

7. CRD NOISE SUPPRESSION BYLAW (SALT SPRING ISLAND) — Bylaw No. 3384
   Status: Current (adopted 2006, upheld by BC Supreme Court 2013)
   URL: https://www.crd.ca/media/file/3384-noise-suppression-bylaw-salt-spring-island-no-1-2006b
   Relevance: MODERATE — Regulates noise on Salt Spring Island.
   Interacts with OCP commercial/tourist use policies (B.3) and
   temporary use permit provisions (G.1).

8. CRD COMMUNITY PARKS REGULATIONS — Bylaw No. 3795
   Status: Current (adopted 2012)
   URL: https://www.crd.ca/media/file/3795-community-parks-regulations-juan-de-fuca-and-salt-spring-island-electoral-areas
   Relevance: MODERATE — Regulates community parks in the Salt Spring Island
   Electoral Area. Interacts with OCP parks and recreation policies (B.7)
   and park land dedication provisions (H.5).

9. CRD FIRE REGULATION BYLAW — Bylaw No. 4489
   Status: Current (adopted 2022)
   URL: https://www.crd.ca/programs-services/bylaw-compliance/crd-regulatory-bylaws
   Relevance: LOW-MODERATE — Fire safety regulations. Interacts with OCP
   natural hazard areas (A.7) and wildfire management.

10. CRD ANIMAL REGULATION AND IMPOUNDING BYLAW — Bylaw No. 1465
    Status: Current (adopted 1986)
    URL: https://www.crd.ca/programs-services/bylaw-compliance/crd-regulatory-bylaws
    Relevance: LOW — Animal control. Minor interaction with agricultural
    land policies.

11. CRD UNSIGHTLY PREMISES BYLAW — Bylaw No. 1887
    Status: Current (adopted 1991)
    URL: https://www.crd.ca/programs-services/bylaw-compliance/crd-regulatory-bylaws
    Relevance: LOW — Property maintenance standards.

=====================================
OTHER RELEVANT DOCUMENTS
=====================================

12. NORTH SALT SPRING WATERWORKS DISTRICT BYLAWS
    Status: Various; operating under the Local Government Act
    URL: https://northsaltspringwaterworks.ca/documents/bylaws/
    Relevance: HIGH — The NSSWD is the largest water purveyor on the island,
    serving the Ganges area. Its bylaws on water allocation, connection,
    and conservation directly affect whether OCP density targets in
    Ganges Village (B.5) can be achieved.

13. AGRICULTURAL LAND COMMISSION ACT & ALR REGULATIONS
    Status: Provincial legislation
    URL: https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/
    Relevance: HIGH — ALR regulations override local land use bylaws.
    The OCP acknowledges this (B.6.2) but contains internal tensions
    regarding non-farm use and park expansion into ALR land.

14. BC RIPARIAN AREAS PROTECTION REGULATION
    Status: Provincial regulation
    URL: https://www.bclaws.gov.bc.ca/
    Relevance: MODERATE — Referenced in OCP DPA 7 (E.7). Sets minimum
    standards for riparian protection that DPA guidelines must meet.
